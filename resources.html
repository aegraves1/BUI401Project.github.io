Want to Learn More about Phishing Schemes?

Phishing Schemes use Social Engineering tactics to get the user to click on the file/link/or whatever is attached

These tactics include:

  • Authority: Someone in charge
  • Intimidation: Something BAD will happen if you don't comply
  • Concensus/Social Proof: What is normally expected, this happened before so its ok
  • Scarcity: something is going to run out or time out. Click Now! Don't Think
  • Urgency: this needs to happen NOW
  • Familiarity: A known website or application or person contacts you
  • Trust: Someone who is safe. From IT or customer service

How do websites keep your passwords secure?

Websites with good security will store your password as a hash

Hashing is when a mathematical operation is used to scramble data so that it cannot be read by someone if it is intercepted. It also cannot be reversed without knowing the hasing algorithm used.

There are several hashing algorithms but some are more secure than others.

An example of this is:

  • Plaintext: Password1
  • Hashed in MD5: 2ac9cb7dc02b3c0083eb70898e549b63
  • Hashed in SHA1: 70ccd9007338d6d81dd3b6271621b9cf9a97ea00
  • Hashed in SHA256: 19513fdc9da4fb72a4a05eb66917548d3c90ff94d5419e1f2363eea89dfee1dd
  • Hashed in SHA512: cbe0cd68cbca3868250c0ba545c48032f43eb0e8a5e6bab603d109251486f77a91e46a3146d887e37416c6bdb6cbe701bd514de778573c9b0068483c1c626aec

What types of malware are there and how are they different?

There are several kinds of malware:

  • Viruses: a virus can reproduce itself but it needs you to run a program
  • Worms: a worm is self-replicating
  • Ransomware: encrypt your files and ask for money to give them back
  • Trojans: looks like a harmless or even beneficial thing and turns out to be malicious
  • Spyware: malware that spies on you
  • Keylogger: tracks and records keystrokes
  • Logic Bomb: installed by a disgruntled employee to execute at a certain time
  • Botnet: a group of bots working together, could orchestrate a Distributed Denial of Service attack
